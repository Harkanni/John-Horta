About Me

I'm a Belgian Architect and Designer. I'm an adaptable and outstanding architect of the Art Nouveau style, who ranks with Henry van de Velde and Paul Hankar as pioneers of modern Belgian architecture. I began studies in architecture in 1873 at the Académie des Beaux-Arts and then at Athénée Royal (1874–77), both in Ghent, Belgium before moving to Brussels in 1881 and attended the Académie Royale des Beaux-Arts, where I was a pupil of the Neoclassical architect Alphonse Balat.

My first independent building, the four-storied Hôtel Tassel in Brussels (1892–93), was among the first Continental examples of Art Nouveau. An important feature was its octagonal hall with a staircase leading to various levels. Other notable works are Hôtel Solvay (1895–1900), notable for the plastic treatment of its facade, Hôtel Winssingers (1895–96), Maison du Peuple, Brussels (1896–99), which was the first structure in Belgium to have a large iron and glass facade as well as my own house on the rue Américaine (1898).
