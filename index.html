VICTOR HORTA

I'm a Belgian Architect and Designer. I'm an adaptable and outstanding architect of the Art Nouveau style, who ranks with Henry van de Velde and Paul Hankar as pioneers of modern Belgian architecture. I began studies in architecture in 1873 at the Académie des Beaux-Arts and then at Athénée Royal (1874–77), both in Ghent, Belgium before moving to Brussels in 1881 and attended the Académie Royale des Beaux-Arts, where I was a pupil of the Neoclassical architect Alphonse Balat.

Frequently Asked Questions

My first real Art Nouveau style work comes from a order from a fellow mason, Emile Tassel. Finished in 1893, the Hotel Tassel, a private mansion, represents the first worldwide example of Art Nouveau in architecture.

It is generally considered the first true Art Nouveau building, because of its highly innovative plan and its groundbreaking use of materials and decoration.

Art Nouveau is characterized by its use of a long, sinuous, organic line and is most often used in architecture, interior design, jewelry and glass design, posters, and illustration

Architects create designs for new construction projects, alterations and redevelopments. They use their specialist construction knowledge and high-level drawing skills to design buildings that are functional, safe, sustainable and aesthetically pleasing.
